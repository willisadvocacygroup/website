The Challenge

What happens each day

Day 1

R — Recognize the Collapse

The first and hardest day. Your only job: name what happened without minimizing it. You'll receive a structured writing exercise that walks you through an honest account of the collapse — including the parts you've been avoiding. You'll end Day 1 with the clearest picture you've had of what actually occurred.

Day 2

E — Evaluate What Remains

Shift from victim accounting to architect inventory. You're going to discover you have more to build with than you think. Day 2's exercise walks you through a values audit, a character inventory, and a "survived" list — the specific evidence of what the collapse could not take from you.

Day 3

B — Believe

Not motivational belief. Evidence-based belief. By the end of Day 3, you will have built a written case — five specific pieces of evidence — that what's ahead of you is possible. The man who has survived what you've survived has already proven something. Day 3 makes that proof explicit.

Day 4

U — Upgrade Your Identity

The reconstruction work begins. You'll identify the specific beliefs and behaviors that made you vulnerable to the collapse — and write the upgraded replacements. Day 4 produces a written "Old Identity → New Identity" document that you'll reference for months.

Day 5

I — Ignite Your Purpose

The collapse revealed something about what you're here to do. Day 5 excavates it. Through a series of directed questions about what makes you angry, what you'd fight for, and what problem in the world feels personal to you, you'll draft a purpose statement — one sentence — by end of day.

Day 6

L — Lead Yourself First

Leadership begins in private. Day 6 builds your daily protocol — the non-negotiable disciplines of the man you're becoming. Not a 47-step morning routine. Three to five specific commitments that, maintained consistently, build the evidence that you lead yourself before you lead anyone else.

Day 7

D — Design Your Legacy

The final day turns everything inward outward. Your rebuild is not just for you. Day 7 produces a written legacy vision — who you're serving, what you're building, and how the pain you survived becomes the credential that makes you the most trusted guide in the room. You'll end Day 7 with a direction, not just a destination.
