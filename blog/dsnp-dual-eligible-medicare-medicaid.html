What Is a D-SNP Plan?

A Dual Special Needs Plan (D-SNP) is a type of Medicare Advantage plan designed specifically for people who receive both Medicare and Medicaid benefits. These plans are approved by CMS (the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) and offered by private insurance companies under contract with Medicare.

Because beneficiaries who qualify for both programs often have complex health needs and limited incomes, D-SNP plans are built to provide significantly more comprehensive coverage than standard Medicare Advantage or Original Medicare.

Who Qualifies for a D-SNP?

Eligibility is straightforward: you must be enrolled in both Medicare (Parts A and B) and a Medicaid program in your state.

In Massachusetts, Medicaid is called MassHealth. If you have MassHealth in addition to Medicare, you are dual eligible and may qualify for a D-SNP.

You may be eligible even if:

  • You receive only limited MassHealth benefits (such as Medicare Savings Program)
  • You have a chronic illness or disability
  • You are under 65 on Medicare due to disability

What Benefits Do D-SNP Plans Include?

Benefits vary by plan and carrier, but here's what D-SNP plans commonly include in Massachusetts:

$0 Monthly Premium No premium beyond your standard Part B payment (which Medicare pays for most dual-eligible beneficiaries).
$0 Primary Care Copays See your doctor at no cost. Many plans also cover specialist visits at $0 or very low cost.
Dental Coverage Cleanings, x-rays, fillings, and in some plans, dentures and extractions — at $0 or low cost.
Vision Coverage Eye exams and an annual allowance toward glasses or contact lenses.
Hearing Benefits Hearing exams and allowances toward hearing aids — often $500–$2,500/year.
Free Transportation Rides to medical appointments, pharmacies, and sometimes dialysis — no cost to you.
OTC Monthly Allowance $25–$150+ per month to spend on approved health products (vitamins, bandages, cold medicine, etc.).
Meal Delivery Some plans include post-hospital meal delivery or chronic condition meal programs.

D-SNP vs. Standard Medicare Advantage: What's the Difference?

Both are types of Medicare Advantage plans. The key differences are:

  • Eligibility: Anyone on Medicare can enroll in standard MA plans. D-SNPs are only for dual-eligible beneficiaries.
  • Enrollment windows: Standard MA plans can only be changed during AEP (Oct 15–Dec 7) or OEP (Jan 1–Mar 31). D-SNP enrollees can switch plans once per calendar quarter — giving you far more flexibility.
  • Benefits: D-SNPs typically offer richer benefits because they're designed for higher-need populations.
  • Coordination: D-SNPs are designed to coordinate your Medicare and Medicaid benefits together, which standard MA plans don't do.

Why Don't More People Know About D-SNP Plans?

This is the question I get asked most after someone discovers they've been eligible for years and never enrolled.

The honest answer: the system doesn't always do a great job of finding the people who need it most.

D-SNPs are not automatically assigned. You have to enroll. And enrollment requires knowing the plan exists, understanding you qualify, and finding a carrier that offers it in your zip code.
