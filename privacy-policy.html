3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your inquiry — contacting you by phone, text, or email to schedule your free consultation
  • To provide insurance brokerage services — researching plan options available in your area and presenting them to you
  • To follow up — sending relevant insurance information, appointment reminders, and plan-change notifications during enrollment periods
  • To improve our services — analyzing how visitors use our website to improve content and user experience
  • To comply with legal obligations — maintaining records as required by applicable insurance regulations
  • To send marketing communications — only with your prior consent, as detailed below
We will never sell your personal information to third-party marketers or data brokers. Your information is used solely to connect you with insurance options and provide brokerage services.

4. TCPA Consent & Communications

By submitting a consultation request form on our website, you expressly consent — consistent with the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) and applicable state laws — to receive:

  • Phone calls (including calls using automatic telephone dialing systems or pre-recorded messages) to the phone number you provide
  • Text messages (SMS) for appointment scheduling, follow-up, and insurance information
  • Email communications related to your inquiry and insurance options

This consent is not a condition of purchasing any insurance product or service. Standard message and data rates may apply for text messages. You may opt out of text messages at any time by replying STOP to any text message from us. You may opt out of email communications by clicking "Unsubscribe" in any email we send.

We may use an AI-powered voice assistant ("Aria") to handle initial inbound calls and missed calls on behalf of Willis Advocacy Group. Aria is a virtual assistant — not a licensed insurance agent — and will identify herself as such at the start of every interaction.

6. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience and collect analytics data. Specifically, we use:

  • Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — tracks page views, session duration, traffic sources, and conversion events (such as form submissions). Data is anonymized and aggregated.
  • Meta Pixel — tracks website visitors for advertising purposes and to measure the effectiveness of our Facebook ads. If you have a Facebook account, Meta may connect this data to your profile per their Data Policy.
  • TrustedForm — records a certificate of consent at the time you submit a lead form, documenting that you agreed to be contacted. This protects both you and us.

Most browsers allow you to control or disable cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some parts of our website. You may also opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

If you believe we have collected information from a minor, please contact us immediately.

No method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we are committed to protecting your data to the best of our ability.
