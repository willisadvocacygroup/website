Original Medicare (Parts A and B) is the federal government program covering hospital and medical services. Medicare Advantage (Part C) is an alternative offered by private insurance companies that must cover everything Original Medicare does — and often adds benefits like dental, vision, hearing, and transportation. Medicare Advantage plans typically have networks of doctors, while Original Medicare lets you see any doctor who accepts Medicare. The right choice depends on your doctors, prescriptions, health needs, and budget. Uhia will walk through both options for your specific situation at no charge.

Medigap, also called Medicare Supplement insurance, is a policy sold by private insurance companies to fill the gaps in Original Medicare — covering things like copayments, coinsurance, and deductibles. Medigap plans are standardized by letter (Plan G, Plan N, etc.) and allow you to see any doctor who accepts Medicare in the country with no network restrictions. They charge a monthly premium but provide very predictable out-of-pocket costs. Medigap is often the best choice for people who travel frequently, see specialists regularly, or want financial certainty. The Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) runs from October 15 through December 7 every year. During this time, you can switch Medicare Advantage plans, switch from Medicare Advantage back to Original Medicare, join or drop a Part D drug plan, or change Part D plans. Changes made during AEP take effect January 1 of the following year. Missing AEP doesn't always mean you're locked in — Special Enrollment Periods exist for qualifying events, and D-SNP plans for dual-eligible beneficiaries often allow monthly enrollment. Guaranteed issue life insurance is a type of whole life policy that cannot deny coverage based on your health. There is no medical exam and no health questions. If you meet the basic age and residency requirements, you are approved — period. The trade-off is a graded benefit period, typically 2–3 years, during which the death benefit is limited (usually premiums plus interest are returned if death occurs in this window). After that period, the full benefit pays out. For many people with serious health conditions who've been declined elsewhere, guaranteed issue is the only path to coverage. Uhia will tell you honestly whether guaranteed issue is your best option or if a standard policy may be available to you.

Final expense insurance, also called burial insurance, is a whole life policy designed to cover end-of-life costs — funeral expenses, outstanding medical bills, and other final debts. Policies typically range from $5,000 to $25,000 in death benefit and are designed to be affordable on a fixed income. Premiums are locked in for life and the policy cannot be cancelled as long as premiums are paid. Many final expense policies are either guaranteed issue or simplified issue (a few health questions but no exam), making them accessible to people with health conditions. Coverage often starts under $30–$50 per month.
