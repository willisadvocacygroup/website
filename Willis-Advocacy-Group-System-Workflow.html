PHASE 04 — IRIS AI QUALIFICATION AGENT

The 60-Second Response Call

🤖

What Iris Does

Iris is an AI phone agent (Synthflow) that calls every new lead within 60 seconds of form submission — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. She qualifies the lead, collects intake information, tells Uhia's story, and books the appointment. By the time Uhia gets on the call, the lead already feels like they know him.

Iris Call Flow

1

Introduction (0:00–0:30)

"Hi, is this [First Name]? This is Iris, calling from Willis Advocacy Group on behalf of Uhia Willis. You just requested a free Medicare review on our website — I'm reaching out right away because we don't let time go by when someone needs help. Do you have about 5 minutes?"

2

Qualification (0:30–2:30)

Collects: Current coverage, state/county, date of birth, Medicaid status (D-SNP qualifier), current plan name, primary doctor. For life insurance: health condition(s), coverage amount desired, previous declines.

3

Uhia's Story — The Warm Transfer (2:30–4:00)

Iris tells Uhia's story in 45 seconds — why he started this, who he fights for, what "People Over Profits" actually means. The lead goes from "who is this person" to "I need to talk to him." This moment is why leads show up to the call ready.

4

Appointment Booking (4:00–5:00)

Iris checks Uhia's GHL calendar in real-time and offers 2 time slots. Lead picks one. Iris confirms: "Perfect. Uhia will call you [day] at [time]. You'll also get a confirmation text and email right now." Appointment is created in GHL and SOA clock starts.

5

Data Pushed to GHL

All collected data (DOB, county, plan name, conditions, appointment time) is pushed back to the GHL contact record as custom fields. Uhia's pre-call brief auto-generates so he walks into every call fully prepared.

If Iris Gets No Answer:
✅ Leaves Voicemail + Sends SMS
Branded voicemail + immediate SMS: "Hi [Name], Iris here from Willis Advocacy Group — I just tried calling about your Medicare review request. Call us back at (774) 446-0701 or reply YES and we'll call you right back."
🔄 Retry Schedule
Attempt 2: 2 hours later
Attempt 3: Next morning 9am
Attempt 4: Next day 2pm
After 4 attempts: Lead enters 14-day nurture sequence automatically.

PHASE 06 — SCOPE OF APPOINTMENT (MANDATORY CMS COMPLIANCE)

Before the Medicare Consultation

⚖️
CMS REQUIREMENT:  A Scope of Appointment must be completed by the beneficiary a minimum of 48 hours before any discussion of specific Medicare Advantage or Part D plans. No exceptions. Violations can result in carrier contract termination and CMS penalties.
1
SOA Triggered Automatically at Booking
When Iris books the appointment or the lead confirms a time slot, GHL automatically sends the digital SOA form via email and SMS. The form includes the beneficiary's name, date, and the scope options (Medicare Advantage, Supplement, Part D, Life Insurance).
📋 Sent immediately upon booking — 48-hour clock starts
2
Lead Signs SOA Electronically
Branded SOA form hosted at willisadvocacygroup.com/soa. Lead signs with name + date. Timestamp recorded. PDF confirmation sent to lead's email. Copy stored in GHL contact record as custom field.
✅ Stored with timestamp — CMS audit-ready
3
Uhia Notified — SOA Received
GHL sends Uhia a notification when SOA is signed. If SOA is NOT signed 24 hours before the appointment, GHL sends an automated reminder to the lead. If unsigned 2 hours before: Uhia is alerted to call manually.
🔔 Uhia notification + automated follow-up if pending
PHASE 07 — THE CONSULTATION CALL

Uhia's Call — The Close

Before the Call — Pre-Call Brief

📊

GHL Auto-Generates a Brief

Uhia sees the lead's name, interest, state, DOB, current coverage, Medicaid status, health conditions (if life), and any notes from Iris's call — all in one view before he dials.

📞
Call is Recorded
GHL call recording active. Opening disclosure: "This call may be recorded for quality and compliance purposes." Required for Medicare sales calls.

Call Structure

1

Rapport (2 min)

Lead already knows Uhia's story from Iris. Confirm what they shared, acknowledge their situation.

2

Needs Assessment (5 min)

Current coverage gaps, doctors they need to keep, budget, priorities. Listen more than talk.

3

Plan Presentation (10 min)

Sunfire Matrix / Connecture: pull real-time plan options. Present 2-3 recommendations max. Explain trade-offs honestly.

4

Decision + Next Step

Lead chooses plan OR schedules follow-up. Either way, pipeline stage updated in GHL immediately.

PHASE 08 — APPLICATION SUBMISSION TO CARRIER

From "Yes" to Submitted Application

📋 Medicare Application Flow

1

Open Sunfire Matrix or Connecture

Pull up the beneficiary's county, DOB, and effective date. Run real-time plan comparison. Confirm selected plan is still available and doctor is in-network.

2

Verify Enrollment Eligibility

Confirm: SEP reason (if applicable), IEP/AEP/OEP window, no other active enrollment, dual eligible status confirmed (for D-SNP), Medicare Part A + B effective dates.

3

Submit Application

Online enrollment via Sunfire, Connecture, or carrier's agent portal. Fields: Medicare ID, name as on Medicare card, address, plan selection, effective date, PCP selection (if required).

4

Confirmation Number Received

Carrier returns confirmation number. Record in GHL contact. Read confirmation number to client verbally. Client will receive welcome kit from carrier within 7-10 days.

5

Post-Enrollment Verification Call

Some carriers require a post-enrollment call from their compliance team within 48 hours. Inform client to expect this call and not to be alarmed.

🛡️ Life Insurance Application Flow

1

Select Carrier Based on Condition

Match health condition(s) to carrier sweet spots. Guaranteed Issue: Gerber Life, AIG, Mutual of Omaha. Simplified Issue: varies by condition. Graded Benefit: case-by-case. Use carrier underwriting guides.

2

Run Quote

Age, gender, state, coverage amount, health classification. Present 2-3 options with monthly premium, benefit amount, graded period (if any), and carrier rating.

3

Complete e-App or Paper App

Most carriers have e-App via agent portal. Collect: full legal name, DOB, SSN, beneficiary name + relationship + DOB, health questions (if simplified issue), payment method (EFT or credit card).

4

Client Signs Electronically

e-Signature via carrier portal. Applicant receives email with policy summary and signed application copy. Policy number assigned upon approval (same day for GI, 1-5 days for simplified).

5

Policy Issued — Record in AgencyBloc

Policy number, carrier, face amount, premium, effective date, and commission rate recorded in AgencyBloc book of business management system.
