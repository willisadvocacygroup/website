### POST 13 — MONDAY (Educational: Medicare Supplement / Medigap)
**Platform:** Facebook + LinkedIn

A lot of people on Original Medicare think their coverage is "good enough."

Until they get a hospital bill.

Original Medicare pays 80% of most costs. That other 20%? It comes out of your pocket — with no cap. No limit. No ceiling.

That's where Medicare Supplement plans (Medigap) come in.

A Medigap policy fills the gap. Depending on the plan, it can cover:
→ The 20% Medicare doesn't pay
→ Hospital stays beyond 60 days
→ Skilled nursing facility coinsurance
→ Foreign travel emergencies

The catch? Medigap has a monthly premium. For some people, that's worth every penny. For others, a Medicare Advantage plan makes more sense.

The right answer depends entirely on YOUR situation — your health, your income, your doctors.

That's why I do free reviews. Not to sell you something. To tell you the truth.

**Platform:** Facebook + LinkedIn

Quick reminder for anyone on Medicare:

The Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) runs October 15 – December 7 every year.

That's the window when you can:
→ Switch from Original Medicare to Medicare Advantage (or back)
→ Change your Medicare Advantage plan
→ Change your Part D drug plan
→ Drop or add coverage

Miss it, and you're locked in for another year.

The difference between the right plan and the wrong plan can be thousands of dollars — in premiums, copays, or drugs not covered.

The best time to start thinking about AEP isn't October 15th. It's NOW.

### POST 16 — MONDAY (Educational: Part D / prescription drug coverage)
**Platform:** Facebook + LinkedIn

This is the Medicare question I get asked constantly:

"Why is my prescription so expensive? I thought Medicare covered drugs."

Here's what a lot of people don't know:

Original Medicare (Part A & B) does NOT cover most prescription drugs. For drug coverage, you need a separate Part D plan — or a Medicare Advantage plan that includes drug coverage.

Part D plans vary widely. The same drug can cost $8 on one plan and $240 on another — depending on your plan's formulary (drug list) and which "tier" your medication falls on.

If you haven't had your drug coverage reviewed recently, there's a good chance you're overpaying. Insurance companies quietly move drugs between tiers every year.

A 20-minute review could save you hundreds. Free of charge.
